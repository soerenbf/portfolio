This design project was done in a course called "Shape-changing interfaces", which had the focus of exploring design possibilities offered in shape-changing concepts.

My group and I decided to explore the concept of spaciality, having one shape which divides into two seperate shapes. We made a lamp which opens opun a person moving in its immediate proximity (~1 meter), providing limited light to the inhabited space. The shape-change and light increase can communicate affordances like accessibility, which could be used in a number of interfaces. An example could be an anwering machine, reading out new messages.

The prototype displayed in the video is the final of two developed prototypes in the course, which spanned over a 7 week period.
